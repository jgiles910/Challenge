Welcome!

Welcome to the Rothko Museum at Bilbao, a space dedicated to immersing yourself in the profound and evocative work of one of the great masters of color of the 20th century! Located in the heart of Biscay, our museum is a haven for art and culture lovers. Founded by passionate collectors Jonathan Giles and Jon Ander, every corner of this museum has been designed to offer a unique experience, where the emotional intensity and complexity of Rothko's works come alive. Join us on this visual and sensory journey that invites reflection and connection with art in its purest form. Explore, feel, and be swept away by Rothko's timeless legacy!

Mark Rothko's story

Mark Rothko, born Marcus Rothkowitz in 1903 in Latvia, is considered one of the most influential artists of the 20th century, famous for his role in the development of Abstract Expressionism. He emigrated to the United States in 1913, settling in Portland, Oregon. Throughout his life, Rothko explored various artistic movements, from Surrealism to Symbolism, but he is especially recognized for his large-scale paintings that utilize simple, dense blocks of color.
His works are a testament to his interest in expressing profound and universal emotions through the pure experience of color and form. Through large compositions of vibrant and often contrasting tones, Rothko seeks to evoke an emotional response in the viewer, inviting contemplative reflection.
