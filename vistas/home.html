Welcome!

The Bauhaus was a revolutionary school of art, design, and architecture founded in 1919 in Weimar, Germany, by the architect Walter Gropius. His core vision was to bridge the gap between "fine arts" and craftsmanship, promoting the idea that "form follows function" to create objects that were both beautiful and accessible for the industrial age. Under Gropius's leadership, the school adopted a unique teaching system of masters and apprentices, attracting iconic figures such as Wassily Kandinsky and Paul Klee. Despite facing constant political pressure that forced it to move to Dessau and finally Berlin, its legacy endured long after it was closed by the Nazi regime in 1933, laying the global foundation for modern design and the International Style we see today.
